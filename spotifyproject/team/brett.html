Bio

When I began this project, I had a few assumptions about my listening habits–some ended up being correct, while others were wrong. I expected to see artists like Playboi Carti, Surf Curse, and Bass Drum of Death in my top five. Those artists have been some of my staples over the past year, and seeing them in concert helped keep them as regulars in my streaming history. However, I wrongly assumed that my average valence (sad songs) would peak in the winter months. I plotted a rolling 30-day average of different audio features for valence, energy, and loudness. It turns out that I was actually listening to more sad music with lower energy and loudness in the months of April and May. This came as a surprise, because I normally associated those months with happy songs and party music to welcome the warmer weather. All in all, this was a great project for learning more about myself. I would recommend anyone curious to know more about their listening habits to go ahead and experiment with the Spotify API.

I liked my winter playlist as well. It featured a lot more rock and metal than my other seasonal playlists. This is where I got another one of my assumptions confirmed. Before pursuing this project, I had a hunch that I tended to listen to more rock and metal during the winter months. Bands like Bass Drum of Death and Slayer appeared in my top 5 artists which definitely contributed to heavier recommendations popping up in the playlist. My data visualizations featuring rolling 30-day averages confirm that the energy levels were very high in the winter months. But having only streamed 2,840 minutes worth of music, this was my least active season on Spotify. That may be attributed to not attending any notable concerts in the winter months.
